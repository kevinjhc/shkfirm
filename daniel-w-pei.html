DANIEL W. PEI

Daniel W. Pei is a litigation attorney and represents individuals and companies in a variety of areas of matters in civil litigation. Daniel grew up in Dalian, China and Rockville, Maryland, and attended Clemson University. After college, Daniel returned to Maryland to attend the University of Baltimore School of Law and graduated with honors. During law school, Daniel was an editor of the University of Baltimore Law Forum, a research assistant for Professor Nancy Modesitt, and a student attorney in the Saul Ewing Civil Advocacy Clinic. After graduation, Daniel served as a law clerk to the Honorable Erik S. Atas in the Circuit Court for Baltimore City, who presided over both civil and criminal matters. Before joining Sung Hwang & Kim, LLP, Daniel was an associate at the Maryland and Washington, D.C. office of a law firm in the D.C. metropolitan area, where he defended attorneys in claims for legal malpractice and in matters before the attorney grievance commission of Maryland, as well as other litigation matters involving the defense of professionals.
